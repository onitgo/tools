What is a domain name?

A domain name is like the address of a shop on the internet. Just as we use a street address to find a specific location in the real world, we use a domain name to find a website on the internet.

For example, when you type www.onitgo.com into your browser, the domain name onitgo.com takes you directly to the official onitgo website, where you can create, listen to, and download your favorite domain names from around the world.

Every website has its own unique domain name, and it helps users find and access content quickly and easily. Whether it's a blog, a news site, an online store, or a social media platform, a domain name is the key to navigating the internet.

Globally, we use the following services in our daily lives as a routine.
  • wikipedia.org is the domain name or address for Wikipedia, a free online encyclopedia that provides articles on a wide range of topics.
  • google.com is the domain name or address for Google, a popular search engine and provider of various online services and products.
  • amazon.com is the domain name or address for Amazon, an online marketplace and provider of various products, services, and digital content.

Types of domain name?

The type of domain name you choose can also have a big impact on your website visibility and branding. Let's take a look to the main types of domain names you can use for your website.

Top-Level Domains (TLDs)

Common TLDs: .com, .org, .net

TLDs are the last part of a domain name and help identify the website's purpose. For example, .com for commercial businesses, .org for organizations, and .net for network services.

Country Code TLDs (ccTLDs)

Examples: .uk, .ca, .de

ccTLDs represent specific countries or regions. For example, .uk for the United Kingdom, .ca for Canada, and .de for Germany.

Sponsored TLDs (sTLDs)

Examples: .aero, .museum

sTLDs are reserved for specific groups or industries. For example, .aero for aviation and .museum for museums.

New gTLDs

Examples: .tech, .store, .blog

New gTLDs offer more specific options for website owners. For example, .tech for tech-related sites, .store for e-commerce sites, and .blog for personal or professional blogs.

Second-Level Domains (SLD)

Example: example.com

The SLD is the part before the TLD. In "example.com," "example" is the second-level domain.

Subdomains

Example: blog.example.com

Subdomains help organize sections of a website. For example, "blog" in "blog.example.com" is a subdomain used for a specific part of the site.

Premium Domain Names

Short, Memorable, and Valuable

Premium domains are short, easy to remember, and often highly valuable for branding, marketing, and search engine optimization.

Vanity Domain Names

Unique & Personalized Domains

Vanity domains are used to create a unique online identity, often with a personal name or slogan that represents a brand, individual, or cause.

Internationalized Domain Names (IDNs)

Domains in Local Languages

IDNs allow websites to have domain names in non-English characters, such as Arabic, Chinese, or Cyrillic. They help businesses connect with local audiences.

E-commerce or Niche Domains

Tailored for Online Stores

These domains are specifically suited for e-commerce websites or niche industries. Examples include .shop for online stores or .fashion for fashion-related businesses.

Expired Domain Names

Available for Re-registration

Expired domains are those that were once registered but are now available for someone else to register. They can carry SEO value if they have backlinks.

Redirect Domain Names

Forwarding to Another Website

Redirect domains send visitors to another website, often used for branding, marketing, or SEO purposes.
